# IT Governance & Automation Control — Policy

## Project #19 | Owner: Felippe | Status: 🔄 In Progress

## What
Draft the formal AI, Automation & Systems Governance Policy establishing IT as the sole owner and approval authority for all automations, scripts, integrations, AI tools, and system-level changes.

## Why
As the company scales, ungoverned automation creates data inconsistencies, compliance risks, and technical debt.

## Deliverable
One formal policy document (SOP format) — ready to distribute to all departments.

## Requirements Checklist

| # | Requirement | Covered by existing SOP? |
|---|-------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | IT is sole owner/approval authority for all automations | No |
| 2 | Departments cannot independently deploy solutions | No |
| 3 | Employees encouraged to identify automation opportunities | No |
| 4 | Employees can use AI for personal productivity | No |
| 5 | Cannot connect automations to company systems without IT | No |
| 6 | Cannot independently purchase/provision AI tools | No |
| 7 | Mandatory IT approval before development | No |
| 8 | Standardized request form (problem, outcome, systems, impact, urgency) | No |
| 9 | IT evaluates based on security, overlap, value, pipeline | No |
| 10 | Approval doesn't guarantee immediate implementation | No |
| 11 | Personal experimentation permitted with non-company accounts | No |
| 12 | Consequences for unauthorized changes | No |
| 13 | Documentation standards for approved automations | Partial — SOP covers how to register systems |

## Status
No open questions. Ready to draft when prioritized.
